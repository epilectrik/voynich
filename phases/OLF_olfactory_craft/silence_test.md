## Synthesis: The Meaningful Silences

**What the manuscript deliberately does NOT encode:**

1. **Smell descriptions** — Because words fail olfactory experience
2. **Heat levels** — Because fixed temperatures destroy volatiles
3. **Duration times** — Because timing varies with conditions
4. **Product names** — Because the operator already knows
5. **Success criteria** — Because quality is purely sensory
6. **Material quantities** — Because sources vary in potency

**Craft-level interpretation:**

These absences are not oversights. They are *exactly* what perfumery cannot
and should not encode in writing:

- Smell cannot be written; it must be experienced
- Heat must be felt, not measured
- Time must be judged, not prescribed
- Quality must be smelled, not described

The manuscript assumes an operator who *already possesses tacit knowledge*.
It provides navigation and warning, not instruction.

**Critical observation:**

If you asked a modern perfumer: *"What can't you write in a recipe book?"*
They would list *exactly these six things*.

The manuscript's silences are the silences of perfumery.
