# Test 2: Historical Failure Account Analysis

**Question:** Do historical distillation operators fear the same things, in the same proportions, as the Voynich hazard distribution?

**Verdict:** STRONG QUALITATIVE MATCH - Historical failure fears align with Voynich hazard hierarchy.

---

## Voynich Hazard Distribution (from FM-PHY-1)

| Hazard Class | Proportion | Meaning |
|--------------|------------|---------|
| **PHASE_ORDERING** | 41% | Material in wrong state/location |
| **CONTAMINATION** | 24% | Impure fractions, mixed products |
| **CONTAINMENT** | 24% | Overflow, pressure, seal failure |
| **RATE_MISMATCH** | 6% | Flow imbalance |
| **ENERGY_OVERSHOOT** | 6% | Thermal damage |

**Key prediction:** If the Voynich hazard distribution reflects real distillation fears, we should find:
1. Phase ordering DOMINANT in historical accounts
2. Contamination and containment SECONDARY and roughly equal
3. Energy treated as a CONTROL VARIABLE, not a primary hazard

---

## Historical Failure Accounts

### Category 1: PHASE ORDERING (Timing/Sequencing Failures)

**Brunschwig - Spirits detection:**
> "When the streaks in the alembic begin to branch, and thick broad drops form in the helmet as if it were sweating on the inside, then the spirits are gone."

Failure mode: Collecting distillate AFTER spirits are exhausted = wrong fraction.

**Fraction separation concern:**
> "The heads of the distillate can contain methanol, a highly toxic substance that can cause permanent blindness and even death."

Failure mode: Failing to discard FIRST fraction = wrong material in product.

**Reinfusion limit:**
> "Reinfusion allowed no more than twice." (Brunschwig)

Failure mode: Exceeding cycle count = quality degradation through wrong sequencing.

**Assessment:** Phase ordering errors are the MOST DISCUSSED failure category in historical sources.

---

### Category 2: CONTAMINATION (Purity Failures)

**Visual contamination markers:**
> "Cloudy material at the bottom of the flask and a reddish tint" (Brunschwig)

**Sludge contamination:**
> "Sludge buildup on the non-heated surfaces can lead to contamination of the distillate as particles from the sludge escape with the solvent vapors."

**Vessel material contamination:**
> John French disapproves of lead vessels "for that they turn the liquors into a white and milky substance besides the malignity they give to them."

**Assessment:** Contamination is a MAJOR secondary concern - product impurity from wrong materials, residues, or vessels.

---

### Category 3: CONTAINMENT (Apparatus Failures)

**Brunschwig's explosion:**
> "Brunschwig includes a first-hand cautionary tale involving exploding stills and knocked-out practitioners."

**Overflow warning:**
> "Vessels filled too full cause overflow or uneven burning." (French)

**Luting failure:**
> "If you do not use lutes, rubber or ground glass joints you are going to lose some of the material you are distilling."

**Seal danger:**
> "Making a completely sealed distillation train is obviously dangerous... Are they all going to condense or are some of them continue to expand and, eventually, burst the apparatus?"

**Assessment:** Containment failures (explosion, overflow, leakage) are a MAJOR secondary concern.

---

### Category 4: RATE MISMATCH (Flow Imbalance)

**Minimal mentions in historical sources.**

No explicit warnings about flow rate imbalance found in surveyed texts. Rate appears to be treated as a consequence of other parameters (fire degree, vessel size) rather than an independent hazard.

**Assessment:** Rate is a MINOR concern in historical accounts - consistent with Voynich 6%.

---

### Category 5: ENERGY OVERSHOOT (Thermal Damage)

**Fourth degree FORBIDDEN:**
> "The fourth degree should be avoided at all times, not just because it threatens to scorch the distilled material, but also because 'it would coerce the thing, which the art of true distillation rejects, because nature too rejects, forbids, and repels all coercion.'"

**Temperature crack warning:**
> "Sudden temperature changes crack glass." (French)

**But energy is primarily a CONTROL VARIABLE:**
- Four degrees of fire = controlled parameter
- Furnace types = operator choice
- Water bath temperature = adjustable ("must not get so hot that one cannot bear to keep a finger in it")

**Assessment:** Energy is treated as a CONTROL VARIABLE with a categorical prohibition (fourth degree), not as a primary hazard source. This matches Voynich's 6% and C490's AGGRESSIVE prohibition pattern.

- Rate would be higher (continuous operation)
