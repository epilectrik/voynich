## Evaluation Criteria

For a material to SURVIVE, it must satisfy ALL of the following:

1. **Process Linkage:** Historically subjected to circulatio, cohobatio, athanor digestion, or quintessence operations
2. **Duration:** Required weeks to months of operation (not hours or days)
3. **Failure Severity:** Failure destroys accumulated work (not just yield)
4. **Restart Catastrophe:** Historical practitioners treated restart as economically or temporally catastrophic
5. **Expert Context:** Required guild-level expertise; not household or commercial-scale

**Automatic Elimination:**
- Primarily aromatic/perfumery
- Culinary or alimentary
- Easily replaceable (cheap base materials)
- Economically scaled (commercial production)
- Safe to discard mid-process
- Endpoint-defined (batch processes with completion criteria)

2. **Material Economics:** Survivors use expensive/scarce materials (gold, mercury); eliminated use cheap/abundant (wine, roses, herbs)

4. **Toxicity as Hazard Proxy:** All survivors are toxic or corrosive; most eliminated are relatively safe
