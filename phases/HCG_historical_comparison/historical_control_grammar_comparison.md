## 3. Cyclic Structure

Each cycle AMPLIFIES - identical to Voynich's hub repetition pattern (22.95x for hub nodes).

All three systems show:
1. Closed-loop cycles that return to origin
2. Multiplication through repetition
3. A dominant cycle structure (4-stages in Voynich, sublimate-fix-dissolve in Geber)

1. ✓ Discrete level control (degrees of fire)
2. ✓ Hard forbidden states (fourth degree rejection)

> **Brunschwig explicitly states that the fourth degree of fire "coerces the thing, which the art of true distillation rejects."**

>

- That the Voynich "is" a distillation manual
- That the author knew Brunschwig or Geber
- That any specific substance is referenced
- That any historical connection exists

### What This DOES Show

The Voynich control grammar is **structurally compatible** with the control grammar implicit in documented medieval distillation traditions. The same architectural principles govern both:

- Discrete states, not continuous parameters
- Hard boundaries, not soft preferences
- Cyclic operations with amplification
- Expert operator with external knowledge
