### Objective 1: Dimensional Lower-Bound Proof

**Question:** Can ≤20 categorical axes reproduce the observed topology?

**Answer:** NO

| Constraint | Why Low-D Fails |

**Conclusion:** Hubs are inevitable geometry under sparse + clustered + navigable constraints.

## What This Phase Established

### 1. The Dimensionality Is Not Arbitrary

The ~128-dimensional MIDDLE space is not a design choice — it is a **structural requirement** imposed by:
- Sparse compatibility (95.7% exclusion)
- Global navigability (96% giant component)
- Hub-mediated routing (5 universal connectors)
- Community structure (PREFIX clustering)

No simpler representation can satisfy all constraints simultaneously.

### 2. The Structure Is Diagnostic

The topology fingerprint (sparse CSP with hubs) is:
- NOT consistent with simple classification
- NOT consistent with random organization
- NOT consistent with mnemonic optimization
- CONSISTENT with high-dimensional physical constraint satisfaction

### 3. Complexity Is Externally Imposed

The anti-mnemonic properties (rare preservation, sparse exclusion, hub rationing, difficulty cycling) demonstrate that the discrimination space is **not a human invention for convenience** but **reflects external physical structure**.
