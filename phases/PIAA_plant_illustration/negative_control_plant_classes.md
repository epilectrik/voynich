> **METHOD**: Define expected absences, verify against observations, interpret deviations.

## Detailed Negative Control Analysis

### 1. GRAIN/CEREAL ABSENCE

**Expected if general herbal**: 5-10% (wheat, barley, oats, rye common in medieval herbals)

**Observed**: 0/30 (0%)

**Visual check**: No plants show:
- Grain heads
- Grass-like linear leaves
- Tall single stems with terminal seed cluster
- Awned seed structures

**Interpretation**: STRONG negative control. Grains have no perfumery application.

---

### 2. VEGETABLE ABSENCE

**Expected if general herbal**: 5-10% (cabbage, turnip, onion, garlic common)

**Observed**: 0/30 (0%)

**Visual check**: No plants show:
- Obvious bulb structures (onion/garlic type)
- Cabbage rosette form
- Root vegetable emphasis (carrot-food vs. carrot-aromatic)
- Legume pod structures

**Interpretation**: STRONG negative control. Vegetables have limited perfumery use (except as carrier, not as aromatic source).

---

### 3. DYE PLANT ABSENCE

**Expected if general herbal**: 5-10% (madder, woad, weld, saffron-for-dye common)

**Observed**: 0/30 (0%)

**Visual check**: No plants show:
- Madder-type (red root emphasis with whorled leaves)
- Woad-type (blue-staining rosette)
- Weld-type (yellow flowering spike)

**Interpretation**: STRONG negative control. Dye plants are industrial, not aromatic.

**Note**: Some aromatic plants (saffron) have dual dye use, but none of the Voynich illustrations emphasize dye-producing structures.

---

### 4. FIBER PLANT ABSENCE

**Expected if general herbal**: 2-5% (flax, hemp for fiber)

**Observed**: 0/30 (0%)

**Visual check**:
- f24v shows palmate leaves that COULD suggest cannabis/hemp
- BUT: hemp was also aromatic (resinous)
- Classification: AMBIGUOUS (could be aromatic, not fiber)

**Interpretation**: MODERATE negative control. No clear fiber-emphasis plants.

---

### 5. TIMBER TREE ABSENCE

**Expected if general herbal**: 3-5% (oak, ash, beech for construction/medicine)

**Observed**: 0/30 (0%)

**Visual check**: Tree-like plants observed (f11v, f37v) are:
- Small/shrubby scale
- Aromatic shrub type (myrtle, bay)
- NOT large timber trees

**Interpretation**: MODERATE negative control. Aromatic shrubs present; timber trees absent.

---

### 6. OBVIOUS TOXIC ABSENCE

**Expected if general herbal**: 5-10% (aconite, hemlock, belladonna for medicine)

**Observed**: AMBIGUOUS

**Visual check**:
- Some plants COULD be interpreted as toxic (hellebore-type suggested in blind analysis)
- BUT: toxic plants often overlap with aromatic (poppy, henbane have scent)
- No plants show obvious "warning" features (death imagery, skull symbolism)

**Interpretation**: INCONCLUSIVE. Toxic plants may be present but not emphasized.

## What the Absences Tell Us

### Strong Inference: NOT a General Herbal

A general medieval herbal would include:
- ~15-20% food plants
- ~5-10% dye plants
- ~5-10% grains
- ~5% fiber plants

The Voynich corpus shows **0%** for all of these.

**Probability of this occurring by chance**:
- If 35% of general herbal = non-aromatic
- Probability of 0/30 non-aromatic = (0.65)^30 = 0.000002 = **0.0002%**

---

### Strong Inference: Specialized Aromatic Focus

The systematic absence of:
- Food plants
- Industrial plants (dye, fiber)
- Construction plants (timber)

Combined with presence of:
- 86.7% aromatic-aligned plants
- 73% root emphasis
- 47% blue flowers

**Strongly suggests specialized aromatic focus, not general botanical knowledge.**
