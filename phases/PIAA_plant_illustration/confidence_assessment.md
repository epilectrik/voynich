> **PURPOSE**: Evaluate overall confidence in the perfumery alignment hypothesis.

> **METHOD**: Multi-factor confidence scoring with explicit uncertainty acknowledgment.

---

## Confidence Framework

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **HIGH** | Multiple independent lines of evidence converge; alternatives ruled out |
| **MEDIUM** | Preponderance of evidence supports conclusion; some alternatives possible |
| **LOW** | Evidence suggestive but not conclusive; multiple interpretations viable |
| **INCONCLUSIVE** | Evidence does not favor any interpretation |

## Threats to Validity

### 1. Sample Size

- **Issue**: 30 folios analyzed out of ~130 botanical pages
- **Mitigation**: Sample is representative across manuscript sections
- **Residual Risk**: LOW - pattern is strong across sample

### 2. Observer Bias

- **Issue**: Analyst aware of perfumery hypothesis before classification
- **Mitigation**: Classifications based on morphological features, not expectation
- **Residual Risk**: MODERATE - blind replication would strengthen

### 3. Stylization Ambiguity

- **Issue**: Stylized drawings could be misclassified
- **Mitigation**: Multiple plausible classes assigned; confidence levels noted
- **Residual Risk**: MODERATE - inherent to medieval illustrations

### 4. Historical Knowledge Gap

- **Issue**: Medieval perfumery plant corpus not fully documented
- **Mitigation**: Major sources consulted; conservative estimates used
- **Residual Risk**: LOW - documented sources cover major plant types

## Caveats

1. **This analysis cannot prove the manuscript's purpose** - only that the botanical illustrations are consistent with perfumery specialization.

2. **Class-level alignment does not imply species identification** - individual plant identifications remain speculative.

3. **Consistency is not proof** - other specialized purposes (medical, pharmaceutical) cannot be ruled out entirely.

4. **Statistical significance assumes representative sampling** - full corpus analysis would strengthen conclusions.
