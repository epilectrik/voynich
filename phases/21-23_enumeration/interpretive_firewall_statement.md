## What This Analysis CANNOT Say

### Forbidden Statements

1. **Identity Claims**
   - ~~"This IS a distillation manual"~~
   - ~~"The Voynich Manuscript describes [PROCESS]"~~
   - ~~"The author was controlling [APPARATUS]"~~

   **Why forbidden:** Similarity in abstract control features does not establish identity. Many different physical systems can have identical control profiles.

2. **Historical Claims**
   - ~~"The author knew about [PROCESS]"~~
   - ~~"This was used in [TIME PERIOD] for [PURPOSE]"~~
   - ~~"This connects to [HISTORICAL TRADITION]"~~

   **Why forbidden:** Control signature matching is ahistorical. Similar control profiles can arise independently across eras and cultures.

3. **Translation Claims**
   - ~~"Token X means [WORD]"~~
   - ~~"This section describes [SUBSTANCE]"~~
   - ~~"The recipe is for [PRODUCT]"~~

   **Why forbidden:** This analysis operates on abstracted control features, not semantic content. No token-level interpretation is possible.

4. **Causal Claims**
   - ~~"The similarity proves..."~~
   - ~~"This establishes that..."~~
   - ~~"We now know that..."~~

   **Why forbidden:** Similarity scores are probabilistic measures, not proofs. High similarity increases plausibility but does not establish causation.

5. **Exclusion Claims**
   - ~~"This cannot be [PROCESS]"~~
   - ~~"[DOMAIN] is ruled out"~~

   **Why forbidden:** Low similarity in the candidate library does not exclude unlisted processes. The library is not exhaustive.
