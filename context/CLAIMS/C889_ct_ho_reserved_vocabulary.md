# C889 - ct-ho Reserved PP Vocabulary

**Tier:** 2 | **Scope:** A | **Phase:** CURRIER_A_STRUCTURE_V2

## Statement

In Section H, MIDDLEs `h`, `hy`, `ho` are almost exclusively ct-prefixed (98-100%), creating a reserved vocabulary for linking/transfer operations at the PP level.

## Evidence

From CURRIER_A_STRUCTURE_V2 test 24:

**MIDDLE Reservation in Section H:**

| MIDDLE | ct-prefixed | Total in H | % Reserved |
|--------|-------------|------------|------------|
| h | 182 | 185 | **98.4%** |
| hy | 96 | 97 | **99.0%** |
| ho | 36 | 36 | **100%** |
| hod | 11 | 12 | 91.7% |

**Comparison with common MIDDLEs:**

| MIDDLE | ct-prefixed | Total in H | % Reserved |
|--------|-------------|------------|------------|
| o | 6 | 436 | 1.4% |
| y | 5 | 446 | 1.1% |

## Token Class Distribution

ct-prefix tokens in Section H:
- PP class: 302 (78.2%)
- RI class: 84 (21.8%)

Most common ct-prefix PP tokens:
- cthy: 95
- cthol: 49
- cthor: 41
- cthey: 28

## Extension of C837

This extends C837 (ct-ho linker signature at RI level) to PP level:

| Level | Evidence | Function |
|-------|----------|----------|
| RI (C837) | 4 linker tokens: ctho, cthody, ctheody, qokoiiin | Mark transferable materials |
| PP (C889) | cthy, cthol, cthor vocabulary | Process instructions for transfers |

The same ct-ho morphological pattern marks linking operations at both RI and PP levels.

## Interpretation

The reserved ct-ho vocabulary at PP level may describe:
- Processes that create "transferable outputs" (materials usable as inputs elsewhere)
- Process steps that reference cross-folio materials
- LINK operations in the programmatic sense (monitoring/intervention boundary)
