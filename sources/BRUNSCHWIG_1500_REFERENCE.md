## Distillation Methods (Part 1)

Brunschwig describes **4 degrees of distillation** (4 grades):

| Degree | Heat Level | Materials | Shelf Life |
|--------|------------|-----------|------------|
| 1st | Gentle (balneum mariae) | Flowers, delicate herbs | 1-2 years |
| 2nd | Moderate | Leaves, most herbs | 2-3 years |
| 3rd | Strong | Roots, barks | 2-3 years |
| 4th | Intense (dry distillation) | Seeds, resins, animals | Varies |

### Key Distillation Equipment
- Alembic (standard distillation)
- Balneum mariae (water bath - gentle)
- Per descensum (downward distillation)
- Cucurbit with receiver

### Example Entry: Andoꝛn waſſer (Horehound)

**Names**: Greek: philoſtoꝛes | Arabic: faraſion/marmaco | Latin: marubium/praſſium | German: Andoꝛn, Gottzvergeßen

**Description**: Two varieties (male/female). Male is dark brown-black like large nettles with 4-sided stem, 2 ells high. Female has round, wrinkled, pale leaves with white round stem, 1 ell high.

**Distillation**: Whole plant (root, stem, herb) chopped and distilled at end of May.

**Uses** (A-P documented):
- A: Cough (drink 2-3 lot morning/evening for several days)
- B: Chest tightness/shortness of breath
- C: Strengthens pregnant women and their children
- D: Good sense and reason (drink 8-10 days, anoint head)
- E: Ear pain (warm, drip into ears)
- F: Spitting blood
- G: Bad dreams/fantasies
- H: Heals wounds (wash wounds)
- I: Heals open ulcers (wash morning/evening)
- K: Dropsy (drink morning/evening, avoid excess drink/moist food)
- L: Strengthens stomach
- M: Strengthens chest
- N: Strengthens lungs and liver
- O: Strengthens kidneys and spleen
- P: Strengthens bladder

## Comparison: Brunschwig vs Puff vs Voynich

| Aspect | Brunschwig 1500 | Puff 1500 | Voynich B |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Unit count** | ~200 waters | 83 chapters | 83 folios |
| **Organization** | Alphabetical by water | Alphabetical by plant | Unknown |
| **Material per unit** | 1 water = 1 material | 1 chapter = 1 plant species | 1 folio = ? |
| **Variants per material** | 10-26 uses (A-Z) per water | 1-3 preparations per chapter | ~21 unique MIDDLEs per folio |
| **Classification system** | 10 categories by nature | Humoral qualities | REGIME (1-4) |
| **Degree system** | 4 distillation degrees | 4 humoral degrees | 4 REGIMEs? |

### Key Observations

1. **Scale difference**: Brunschwig has ~200 materials vs Puff's 83 vs Voynich's 83
   - But Puff's 83 is "simples" (single plants)
   - Brunschwig includes compounds, animal products, and multiple plant parts

2. **Variants structure**:
   - Brunschwig: 10-26 therapeutic uses per water (A-Z system)
   - Voynich B: ~21 unique MIDDLEs per folio
   - Similar order of magnitude for "outputs per material"

3. **Classification overlap**:
   - Both use 4-degree systems
   - Both have material→process categorical mappings
   - Both distinguish hot/cold, moist/dry

4. **If we count only herbal simples in Brunschwig**: ~150 distinct plant species
   - Still larger than Puff's 83
   - Puff explicitly limited scope to "most useful" simples

## Shelf Life Summary

| Duration | Material Types |
|----------|----------------|
| 1 year | Fruits, moist roots, most animals |
| 2 years | Cold/moist flowers, moderate herbs, hot/dry roots |
| 3 years | Hot flowers, leaves, hot/dry herbs |
| 5-6 years | Honey water (special case) |

Note: All waters must be "rectified" (redistilled in sunlight) annually to maintain potency.
